Commercially: yes, mostly.

The original MVP was borderline “premium Excel”.

The enhanced version becomes:
	•	Decision-support tool
	•	Behavioural framing tool
	•	Emotional reassurance tool
	•	Ongoing tracking tool

That absolutely increases willingness to pay.

The key improvements are:
	•	Milestone timeline (psychological impact)
	•	Spending impact ranking (actionable clarity)
	•	Decision comparison (take job now vs wait)

However —

Two of the enhancements cross into risk territory if not handled carefully:
	•	Decision Matrix framing
	•	Action prompts tied to financial consequences

That’s where liability risk increases.

⸻

2️⃣ Is This Creating Financial Advice Liability?

Short answer:

It can, depending on wording.

The line between:

“Illustrative modelling tool”
and
“Financial advice / regulated guidance”

is not about complexity.

It is about:
	•	Personalised recommendations
	•	Implied suitability assessment
	•	Directive language

⸻

🚨 Risk Areas in the Enhanced Version

Let’s examine the dangerous areas.

⸻

⚠️ A) Decision Matrix (“Should I take lower job?”)

This is close to advice if you say:

“Option A is better.”
“You should accept.”
“This is optimal.”

That would be problematic.

But if you:
	•	Present financial impact only
	•	Show runway delta
	•	State trade-offs neutrally
	•	Avoid normative conclusions

Then you are still in modelling territory.

Safe framing:

“Financial impact of each option under your assumptions.”

Not safe framing:

“Best choice for you.”

⸻

⚠️ B) Spending Optimizer

This becomes risky if:

You prescribe what to cut.

Safe:

“If reduced by £45/month, runway increases by 18 days.”

Not safe:

“You should cancel this.”

You must avoid ranking with language implying behavioural prescription.

Even “Quick Wins” can feel advisory if not neutral.

⸻

⚠️ C) Milestone Triggers (“Contact lender”)

This is the most sensitive area.

Saying:

“Consider contacting your lender regarding payment holiday options”

is borderline but usually fine.

Saying:

“You need to contact your lender”

is advisory.

You must phrase as:

“This projection suggests that under these assumptions, capital falls below £X by Week Y.”

Avoid instructing actions.

⸻

⚠️ D) Sector Intelligence

This is relatively low risk.

As long as:
	•	You cite public sources
	•	You state it is historical averages
	•	You avoid predicting individual outcomes

This is safe.

⸻

3️⃣ FCA / Regulatory Perspective

In the UK, you enter regulated territory if you:
	•	Recommend specific financial products
	•	Advise on suitability
	•	Provide personalised financial advice
	•	Guide on debt restructuring strategies

You are NOT:
	•	Recommending lenders
	•	Selling insurance
	•	Structuring debt instruments
	•	Advising on investments

You are modelling cash flow.

That is generally unregulated.

But:

The more your tool says “do X” rather than “if X then Y”, the more risk increases.

⸻

4️⃣ The Real Liability Risk Is Misrepresentation

Not regulation.

The biggest risk is:

A user claims:

“I relied on this tool’s guidance and made a decision.”

Your defence must be:
	•	Clear disclaimers
	•	No prescriptive language
	•	Explicit statement: illustrative modelling only
	•	Clear assumption display
	•	No guarantees

⸻

5️⃣ Does the Enhanced Version Increase Risk?

Yes — slightly.

Because:

It moves from “calculator” to “decision-support system”.

Decision-support is fine.

Decision-making instruction is not.

The implementation must be extremely neutral.

⸻

6️⃣ How To Keep It Safe

Replace:

“Should I take this job?”
With
“Financial comparison of two income scenarios.”

Replace:

“Quick Wins”
With
“Runway Impact of Selected Expense Adjustments.”

Replace:

“Critical Action Required”
With
“Projected Capital Threshold Reached.”

Replace:

“Consider mortgage holiday”
With
“Mortgage payment represents X% of expenses in this scenario.”

Keep everything:
	•	Quantitative
	•	Neutral
	•	User-driven

Never instruct.

⸻

⚠️ Must avoid “advisor tone”

Proceed.

But implement with:
	•	Clinical language
